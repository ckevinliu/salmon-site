Salmon vs ZoomInfo

You're paying for a database.
We do the research.

ZoomInfo sells you a snapshot of who people were. Salmon tells you who they are right now — and finds the ones you didn't know to ask for.

The real problem

B2B data decays the moment it's exported.

ZoomInfo's model was built for a world where data moved slowly. Quarterly refreshes made sense when people stayed at companies for years.

That world is gone. 30-40% of B2B contact data goes stale every year. Job changes, reorgs, new titles — your CRM is a graveyard of who these people used to be.

When you buy ZoomInfo, you're buying a frozen moment in time. Salmon doesn't have a database — we research in real time, every time you ask.

The bottom line

Choose based on what you're actually buying.

ZoomInfo works if you need a large, fast database of rough-cut records and you're running high-volume outbound where contact decay is an acceptable tax.

If accuracy and freshness actually matter to your business — if a wrong title costs you a deal, if a bad number burns a relationship, if your compliance team needs to trust the data — ZoomInfo will disappoint you.

Salmon is built for teams who need to trust what they're working with. Real-time research means you get the truth about a contact today, not who they were six months ago.

ZoomInfo is better for

High-volume, low-precision outbound

If you're blasting thousands of contacts and bounce rate is a tax you've accepted, ZoomInfo's scale and speed makes sense. Freshness is the tradeoff.

Salmon is better for

Every situation where accuracy matters

Complex research, CRM hygiene, mid-market deals, compliance use cases — any workflow where stale data costs you real money. Salmon researches. ZoomInfo retrieves.
