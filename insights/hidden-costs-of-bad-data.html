The Hidden Costs of Bad Data (and How to Fix Them)

Salmon Team Oct 30, 2025 3 min read

Bad data isn't just a minor inconvenience — it's a significant barrier on your sales team's productivity, performance, and morale. But by understanding the true costs and embracing a better way forward, you can unlock your team's full potential and drive sustainable business growth.

The Financial Impact

Let's start with the most obvious cost: wasted time. When your BDRs are spending hours each day verifying contact information, they're not doing what they're best at — building relationships and closing deals. In fact, for a team of just 10 BDRs, bad data can cost you a staggering $375,000 annually in lost productivity.

But it's not just about the time your team is losing. It's about the opportunities they're missing. Every hour spent on data janitorial work is an hour that could have been spent on revenue-generating activities. Over the course of a year, that can add up to millions in lost pipeline and revenue.

The Human Toll

The costs of bad data extend far beyond the financial. You didn't hire BDRs to verify phone numbers. You hired them to build relationships and create opportunities. Every hour they spend on data cleanup represents an hour they're not doing what they're actually good at.

When your BDRs can't trust the data they're working with, they become frustrated, demotivated, and disengaged. They start to question the value of their work and their ability to succeed in their role.

Over time, this can lead to higher turnover rates, as your top performers seek out opportunities where they can make a real impact. And with each departure, you lose not only a skilled salesperson but also the relationships and institutional knowledge they've built over time.

The impact of poor data quality extends beyond your sales team, affecting multiple aspects of your business. When your team consistently works with inaccurate information, it can compromise the effectiveness of your marketing campaigns, hinder the efficiency of your customer success efforts, and lead to discrepancies in financial forecasting.

What is bad data costing you?

A Better Way Forward

The good news is that there's a solution. By investing in automated data verification and enrichment, you can ensure that your BDRs always have access to accurate, up-to-date information. That means less time spent on manual data cleanup and more time spent on high-value activities.

But the benefits go beyond just productivity gains. When your team can trust their data, they're able to build stronger, more authentic relationships with your customers. They're able to tailor their outreach to each individual's needs and preferences, leading to higher response rates, more qualified meetings, and ultimately, more closed deals.

The Path to Success

Embracing data quality isn't just a nice-to-have — it's a strategic imperative for any sales organization that wants to stay competitive in today's market. By prioritizing data accuracy and reliability, you can create a culture of success that permeates every level of your organization.

You'll see the results not just in your pipeline and revenue numbers but in the satisfaction and engagement of your sales team. They'll be able to work more efficiently, perform at a higher level, and find greater fulfillment in their roles.

So if you're ready to unlock your team's full potential and drive sustainable business growth, it's time to take a hard look at your data quality. Because with the right data powering your sales efforts, there's no limit to what you can achieve.
